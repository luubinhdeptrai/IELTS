1. Learn 10 phrasal verbs per day.
2. Use the Quick Master List for fast review.
3. Use the Full Meaning Map when a phrasal verb has more than one common meaning.
4. Review confusing pairs before doing exercises.
5. Say the memory sentences out loud and test yourself from Vietnamese to English.

## Confusing Phrasal Verbs

### Wake up vs Get up

- **Wake up** = stop sleeping / make someone stop sleeping
- **Get up** = leave bed / stand up

Memory:
- Wake up = eyes open; get up = body moves

Example:
- I wake up at 7, but I get up at 7:30.

### Catch up vs Catch up on

- **Catch up** = talk with someone or reach the same level
- **Catch up on** = do or learn what you missed

Memory:
- Catch up with a friend; catch up on emails

Example:
- I caught up with Mai, then caught up on my messages.

### Get back to a task vs Get back to someone

- **Get back to a task** = return to doing it
- **Get back to someone** = reply later

Memory:
- Task returns; reply returns

Example:
- I will get back to my report and get back to you tomorrow.

### Check in: Contact vs Hotel

- **Check in with someone** = contact briefly
- **Check in at a hotel/airport** = register arrival

Memory:
- Message vs front desk

Example:
- I checked in with my manager before I checked in at the hotel.

### Pick up: Buy/Get vs Collect vs Lift

- **Pick up something** = buy/get or lift it
- **Pick someone up** = collect someone by car

Memory:
- Hand picks up; car picks up

Example:
- I picked up coffee, then picked up my sister.

### Take out: Remove vs Trash vs Invite

- **Take out something** = remove it
- **Take out the trash** = put rubbish outside; **take someone out** = invite them out

Memory:
- Out of a place, bin, or house

Example:
- Take out the batteries, then take out the trash.

### Put on: Clothes vs Weight vs Event vs Media

- **Put on clothes** = wear them
- **Put on weight/music/a show** = gain weight, start music, or organize a show

Memory:
- Things go onto body, sound, or stage

Example:
- Put on your jacket and put on some music.

### Get over: Illness vs Bad Experience

- **Get over an illness** = recover physically
- **Get over a breakup/problem** = recover emotionally

Memory:
- Climb over sickness or sadness

Example:
- She got over the flu, but not the breakup.

### Go through: Review vs Experience

- **Go through a list** = review step by step
- **Go through a hard time** = experience difficulty

Memory:
- Move through paper or pain

Example:
- Let's go through the plan before we go through another stressful week.

### Look up vs Look up to

- **Look up information** = search for it
- **Look up to someone** = respect someone

Memory:
- Dictionary vs admiration

Example:
- I looked up the word because I look up to my teacher.

### Turn out: Result vs Attendance

- **Turn out well** = have a good result
- **Turn out for an event** = attend

Memory:
- Result appears; people appear

Example:
- The event turned out well because many people turned out.

### Show up: Arrive vs Become Visible

- **Show up** = arrive
- **Show up on a screen/test** = become visible

Memory:
- Person appears; detail appears

Example:
- He showed up late, and the error showed up again.

### Drop off: Leave vs Decrease vs Fall Asleep

- **Drop off a package** = leave it somewhere
- **Sales drop off** = decrease; **drop off** = fall asleep

Memory:
- Object drops there; numbers drop down; eyes close

Example:
- I dropped off the parcel, then almost dropped off on the bus.

### Hold on vs Hold on to

- **Hold on** = wait
- **Hold on to something** = keep holding it

Memory:
- Time pause vs hand grip

Example:
- Hold on a second and hold on to the rail.

### Break down: Machine vs Emotion vs Analysis

- **Break down** = a machine stops working
- **Break down** = become emotional; **break down costs** = divide into parts

Memory:
- Car stops; person cries; numbers split

Example:
- The car broke down, so we broke down the costs of repair.

### Get through vs Pull through

- **Get through** = finish/survive a difficult period or task
- **Pull through** = survive something serious, often illness or danger

Memory:
- Tunnel vs hospital recovery

Example:
- I got through exam week, and my grandfather pulled through surgery.

### Go on vs Carry on

- **Go on** = continue or happen
- **Carry on** = continue after interruption

Memory:
- Keep talking vs keep working

Example:
- Please go on; I will carry on with my notes.

### Come across vs Come across as

- **Come across** = find/meet by chance
- **Come across as** = give an impression

Memory:
- Find an old photo vs seem friendly

Example:
- I came across his profile, and he came across as polite.

### Take up: Hobby vs Time/Space

- **Take up running** = start running
- **Take up space/time** = use space/time

Memory:
- New hobby vs big sofa

Example:
- I took up yoga, but it takes up a lot of time.

### Turn on vs Turn off vs Switch off

- **Turn on** = start power
- **Turn off / switch off** = stop power

Memory:
- On starts; off stops

Example:
- Turn on the fan, then switch it off before you leave.

### Get on vs Get off

- **Get on** = board transport
- **Get off** = leave transport

Memory:
- Step onto vs step off

Example:
- Get on bus 22 and get off at the market.
