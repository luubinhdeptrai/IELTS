# 100 Daily Phrasal Verbs — Easy Memory Version

## How to Use This File

1. Learn 10 phrasal verbs per day.
2. Cover the English side and recall from Vietnamese.
3. Review confusing pairs before doing exercises.
4. Say the memory sentences out loud.
5. Repeat old days before learning new ones.

## Confusing Phrasal Verbs

### Wake up vs Get up

- **Wake up = stop sleeping**
- **Get up = leave the bed**

Memory:
- Wake up = eyes open
- Get up = body leaves bed

Example:
- I wake up at 7, but I get up at 7:30.

### Catch up vs Catch up on

- **Catch up = talk and share news**
- **Catch up on = do or learn what you missed**

Memory:
- Catch up with a friend
- Catch up on emails

Example:
- I caught up with Mai, then caught up on my messages.

### Get back to a task vs Get back to someone

- **Get back to a task = return to doing it**
- **Get back to someone = reply later**

Memory:
- Task returns
- Reply returns

Example:
- I will get back to my report and get back to you tomorrow.

### Check in: Contact vs Hotel

- **Check in with someone = contact briefly**
- **Check in at a hotel = arrive and receive your room**

Memory:
- Friendly message
- Hotel front desk

Example:
- I checked in with my manager before I checked in at the hotel.

### Break down: Machine vs Emotion

- **Break down = a machine stops working**
- **Break down = someone becomes very emotional**

Memory:
- Car stops
- Person cries

Example:
- The car broke down, and she almost broke down too.

### Get through vs Pull through

- **Get through = finish or survive a difficult period**
- **Pull through = survive something serious, often illness or danger**

Memory:
- Tunnel
- Hospital recovery

Example:
- I got through the exam week, and my grandfather pulled through the surgery.

### Go on vs Carry on

- **Go on = continue, often in speech**
- **Carry on = continue after interruption**

Memory:
- Keep talking
- Keep working

Example:
- Please go on. You can carry on with your work after the call.

### Come across vs Come across as

- **Come across = find by chance**
- **Come across as = give an impression**

Memory:
- Find an old photo
- Seem friendly

Example:
- I came across his profile, and he came across as polite.

### Take up: Hobby vs Time/Space

- **Take up = start a hobby**
- **Take up = use time or space**

Memory:
- New hobby
- Big sofa

Example:
- I took up yoga, but it takes up a lot of time.

### Turn on vs Turn off vs Switch off

- **Turn on = start a device**
- **Turn off / switch off = stop a device**

Memory:
- On = power starts
- Off = power stops

Example:
- Turn on the fan, then switch it off before you leave.

### Get on vs Get off

- **Get on = board a bus, train, plane, or bike**
- **Get off = leave a bus, train, plane, or bike**

Memory:
- Step onto
- Step off

Example:
- Get on bus 22 and get off at the market.
