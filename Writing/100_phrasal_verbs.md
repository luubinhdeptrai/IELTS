- **Vietnamese meaning:** Thức dậy

## Confusing Phrasal Verbs
### Wake up vs Get up

- **Wake up:** stop sleeping.
- **Get up:** leave the bed.

Example:
- I wake up at 7, but I get up at 7:30.

### Catch up vs Catch up on

- **Catch up with someone:** talk after not seeing someone for a while.
- **Catch up on something:** do or learn the latest work, news, or information.

Example:
- I caught up with my friend and caught up on the latest news.

### Get back to vs Get back

- **Get back to a task:** return to doing something.
- **Get back to someone:** reply later.

Example:
- I need to get back to work, but I'll get back to you tomorrow.

### Check in as Contact vs Check in as Hotel

- **Check in with someone:** contact someone briefly.
- **Check in at a hotel:** arrive and officially receive your room.

Example:
- I checked in with my manager before I checked in at the hotel.

### Break down as Machine vs Break down as Emotion

- **A machine breaks down:** it stops working.
- **A person breaks down:** they become very emotional.

Example:
- My car broke down, and I almost broke down too.

### Take up as Hobby vs Take up as Time or Space

- **Take up a hobby:** start doing it.
- **Take up time/space:** use time or space.

Example:
- I took up painting, but it takes up a lot of space.

### Come across as Find vs Come across as Impression

- **Come across something:** find it by chance.
- **Come across as + adjective:** give an impression.

Example:
- I came across her article, and she came across as very confident.
