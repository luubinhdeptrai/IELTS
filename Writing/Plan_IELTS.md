# Seven-Month IELTS Study Plan for an Overall Band Score of 7.0

## Target Scores

- **Listening:** 7.5
- **Reading:** 7.5
- **Writing:** 6.5
- **Speaking:** 6.5
- **Overall:** 7.0

The average of these four scores is:

[
\frac{7.5 + 7.5 + 6.5 + 6.5}{4} = 7.0
]

This target is achievable within seven months if I study consistently, analyse my mistakes carefully, and receive regular feedback, especially for Writing and Speaking.

---

# 1. Recommended Weekly Study Time

I should study approximately **18–21 hours per week**.

A suitable schedule is:

- **3 hours per day**
- **6 days per week**
- **1 rest or light-study day**

I should not divide my study time equally among the four skills. Writing and Speaking usually require more time because they involve producing language and receiving feedback.

| Skill     |  Weekly time | Percentage |          Target |
| --------- | -----------: | ---------: | --------------: |
| Writing   |      6 hours |        30% |             6.5 |
| Speaking  |      5 hours |        25% |             6.5 |
| Listening |    4.5 hours |      22.5% |             7.5 |
| Reading   |    4.5 hours |      22.5% |             7.5 |
| **Total** | **20 hours** |   **100%** | **Overall 7.0** |

Although my target scores for Listening and Reading are higher, these receptive skills can usually improve more predictably through regular practice and detailed error analysis.

Writing and Speaking often improve more slowly because I need to correct grammar, vocabulary, organisation, fluency, and pronunciation problems.

# 2. Recommended Weekly Schedule

## Monday: Writing and Listening

### Writing — 90 minutes

- Study one IELTS Writing Task 2 question type.
- Analyse the question carefully.
- Create an outline.
- Write one body paragraph or a complete essay.
- Check grammar, vocabulary, coherence, and idea development.
- Rewrite weak sentences or paragraphs.

### Listening — 75 minutes

- Complete one or two Listening sections under timed conditions.
- Check the answers.
- Listen again without reading the transcript.
- Identify the reason for each mistake.
- Read the transcript and note useful paraphrases.
- Listen again after analysing the mistakes.

### Vocabulary review — 15 minutes

- Review vocabulary from the Writing and Listening lessons.
- Create example sentences instead of only memorising definitions.

---

## Tuesday: Reading and Speaking

### Reading — 90 minutes

- Complete one Reading passage in approximately 20 minutes.
- Check the answers.
- Find the exact evidence for every answer.
- Analyse why each incorrect option is wrong.
- Record important paraphrases.

### Speaking — 75 minutes

- Practise five to seven Part 1 questions.
- Record one Part 2 answer.
- Answer three or four related Part 3 questions.
- Listen to the recording.
- Correct grammar, pronunciation, fluency, and vocabulary problems.
- Answer the same questions again more naturally.

### Pronunciation practice — 15 minutes

- Practise word stress.
- Practise sentence stress.
- Shadow short sections from podcasts or IELTS recordings.

---

## Wednesday: Writing and Listening

### Writing — 90 minutes

Focus on Academic Writing Task 1.

- Spend five minutes analysing the chart, graph, table, map, or process.
- Identify the most important features.
- Write the introduction.
- Write a clear overview.
- Organise the details into logical paragraphs.
- Check comparisons, numbers, verb tenses, and grammar.

### Listening — 75 minutes

Focus on one weak question type, such as:

- Multiple choice
- Matching
- Map labelling
- Form completion
- Note completion
- Sentence completion
- Diagram labelling

### Vocabulary review — 15 minutes

- Review topic-specific vocabulary.
- Review common Listening spelling mistakes.

# 4. How to Improve Listening to Band 7.5

I should not simply complete Listening tests and count my correct answers.

For every Listening exercise, I should follow this process:

1. Complete the test under timed conditions.
2. Check my answers.
3. Listen again to the sections containing mistakes.
4. Identify why I missed each answer.
5. Read the transcript.
6. Highlight paraphrases and distractors.
7. Listen again without the transcript.
8. Shadow difficult sentences aloud.

I should classify Listening mistakes as:

- I did not know the vocabulary.
- I recognised the word but could not understand its pronunciation.
- I heard the answer but spelled it incorrectly.
- I missed a plural ending.
- I selected a distractor.
- I lost concentration.
- I read the next question too slowly.
- I could not recognise a paraphrase.

### Important Listening areas

- Singular and plural nouns
- Spelling
- Numbers and dates
- Changes of opinion
- Synonyms and paraphrases
- Distractors
- Different English accents
- Connected speech
- Word stress

Before taking the real examination, I should achieve my target Listening score in at least four or five consecutive practice tests.

# 5. How to Improve Reading to Band 7.5

For every incorrect Reading answer, I should answer three questions:

1. Where is the exact evidence in the passage?
2. How was the question paraphrased?
3. Why is my chosen answer incorrect?

I should not only read the answer explanation and move on.

### Create a paraphrase notebook

| Question wording       | Passage wording       |
| ---------------------- | --------------------- |
| a significant increase | rose considerably     |
| harmful consequences   | adverse effects       |
| difficult to maintain  | unsustainable         |
| people living nearby   | local residents       |
| a reduction in cost    | became less expensive |
| important evidence     | significant proof     |

### Suggested time allocation

- Passage 1: 15–17 minutes
- Passage 2: approximately 20 minutes
- Passage 3: 23–25 minutes

I should practise completing the entire Reading test within 60 minutes because there is no additional time for transferring answers.

# 6. How to Improve Writing to Band 6.5

Writing practice should follow a three-stage cycle.

## Stage 1: Write

Complete the task under official time limits:

- Task 1: approximately 20 minutes
- Task 2: approximately 40 minutes

## Stage 2: Receive Feedback

Evaluate the writing according to the four assessment criteria:

- Task Achievement or Task Response
- Coherence and Cohesion
- Lexical Resource
- Grammatical Range and Accuracy

## Stage 3: Rewrite

- Rewrite the entire essay or report.
- Alternatively, rewrite the weakest paragraphs.
- Correct repeated grammar mistakes.
- Improve unclear explanations.
- Replace unnatural vocabulary.
- Improve paragraph organisation.

Rewriting is essential because simply reading corrections does not guarantee that I can produce the correct language independently.

It is better to write two carefully reviewed and rewritten essays per week than seven essays without proper correction.

### Priorities for Band 6.5 Writing

- Answer every part of the question.
- Present a clear position.
- Use relevant main ideas.
- Explain ideas sufficiently.
- Include appropriate examples.
- Organise paragraphs logically.
- Use linking devices naturally.
- Produce a mixture of simple and complex sentences.
- Reduce frequent grammar mistakes.
- Use accurate vocabulary rather than unnecessarily advanced vocabulary.

I do not need extremely rare or complicated vocabulary to achieve Band 6.5.

# 7. How to Improve Speaking to Band 6.5

I should practise speaking aloud every day, even if I only have 15–20 minutes.

## Part 1 Answer Structure

A useful structure is:

> Direct answer → reason → brief detail or example

Example:

> Yes, I enjoy studying in libraries because they provide a quiet environment. I usually go to my university library when I need to concentrate on difficult assignments.

## Part 2 Answer Structure

I can organise my answer using:

1. Introduction
2. Background
3. Main details
4. Feelings or opinions
5. Final reflection

## Part 3 Answer Structure

A useful structure is:

> Position → explanation → example → contrast or qualification

Example:

> I believe public transportation should be subsidised because affordable fares encourage people to use buses and trains. For example, students and low-income workers may depend on public transport every day. However, the government must also ensure that the service remains reliable and safe.

### Speaking review questions

After recording myself, I should ask:

- Did I answer the question directly?
- Did I develop my answer?
- Did I pause too frequently?
- Did I repeat the same words?
- Did I use memorised expressions unnaturally?
- Did I make repeated grammar mistakes?
- Was my pronunciation understandable?
- Did I speak at a natural speed?

I should not memorise complete model answers because memorised responses may sound unnatural and may not answer the exact question.
