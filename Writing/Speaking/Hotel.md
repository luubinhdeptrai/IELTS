# 0. Standardized Source Content

## Part 1: Introduction and Making a Reservation

This lesson is about how to talk about staying at a hotel in English. When you travel, you often need to book a room, check in, ask questions, solve problems, request services, and check out.

### Making a Reservation

Useful source sentences:

* "I'd like to book a room."
* "I'd like to book a room for two nights."
* "I'd like to make a reservation."
* "I need a room for two people from Friday to Sunday."
* "Do you have any rooms available?"

Key explanation:

* A `reservation` is a booking made in advance.
* `Available` means free and ready to use.
* A `double room` usually has one large bed for two people.
* A `twin room` usually has two separate beds.

### Common Pre-arrival Questions

* "Does the room include breakfast?"
* "Is Wi-Fi included?"
* "Is there free parking?"
* "Does the room have air conditioning?"
* "How much is the room per night?"
* "What's the rate per night?"
* "Is breakfast included in the rate?"

## Part 2: Arriving and Checking In

Useful source sentences:

* "Hi, I have a reservation under the name Leo."
* "Hi, do you have any rooms available for tonight?"
* "May I see your ID, please?"
* "Could I have your passport, please?"
* "Sure, here you go."
* "What time is checkout?"
* "Is early check-in possible?"
* "Can I check in a little early?"
* "Is late checkout possible?"
* "Can I have a late checkout?"

Key explanation:

* `Check-in` is when you arrive and receive your room.
* `Checkout` is when you leave and return the key or card.
* `I have a reservation under the name...` means the booking is listed with your name.

## Part 5: Key Source Vocabulary

* **reservation**: a booking made in advance
* **available**: free and ready to use
* **check in**: arrive at a hotel and officially receive your room
* **check out**: leave the hotel and return your room key or card
* **rate**: the price, usually per night
* **housekeeping**: the hotel staff who clean rooms and bring supplies
* **lobby**: the main public area near the hotel entrance
* **luggage**: travel bags and suitcases
* **receipt**: a document showing payment
* **charge**: an amount of money added to a bill
* **overall**: in general, thinking about everything together

# 1. Topic Overview

This hotel topic covers the real speaking situations you need most when travelling:

* booking a room
* asking about prices, breakfast, Wi-Fi, and facilities
* checking in
* requesting items or services
* reporting room problems politely
* asking for directions inside the hotel
* storing luggage or extending a stay
* checking out and asking about the bill

The goal is not to memorise the whole transcript. The goal is to master the small set of sentences that solve real hotel problems quickly and politely.

# 2. Tier 1 — Essential English

## 1. I'd like to book a room for two nights.

**Vietnamese:** Tôi muốn đặt một phòng trong hai đêm.

**When to use:** Making a hotel reservation.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> I'd like to + verb...

**Usage note:** `I'd like to...` is polite, natural, and safer than `I want to...` at a hotel.

## 2. Do you have any rooms available for tonight?

**Vietnamese:** Tối nay khách sạn còn phòng trống không?

**When to use:** Asking if a room is free, especially without a booking.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> Do you have any + plural noun + available?

**Usage note:** `Available` means free and ready to use.

## 3. I have a reservation under the name Leo.

**Vietnamese:** Tôi có đặt phòng dưới tên Leo.

**When to use:** Checking in when you already booked a room.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> I have a reservation under the name + name.

**Usage note:** Replace `Leo` with your own name.

## 4. Is breakfast included in the rate?

**Vietnamese:** Bữa sáng có được bao gồm trong giá phòng không?

**When to use:** Asking whether breakfast is part of the room price.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> Is + noun + included in the rate?

**Usage note:** `Rate` means the price for the room, usually per night.

## 5. What time is checkout?

**Vietnamese:** Mấy giờ trả phòng?

**When to use:** Asking when you must leave the room.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> What time is + noun?

**Usage note:** You may also hear `checkout time`.

## 6. Could I have another pillow?

**Vietnamese:** Tôi có thể xin thêm một cái gối khác không?

**When to use:** Requesting something politely.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> Could I have + noun?

**Usage note:** This is more polite than `Give me...`.

## 7. The air conditioner isn't working.

**Vietnamese:** Máy điều hòa không hoạt động.

**When to use:** Reporting a problem with equipment.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> The + noun + isn't working.

**Usage note:** You can replace the noun: `The Wi-Fi isn't working.`

## 8. Hi, I'm staying in room 304 and I have a problem.

**Vietnamese:** Xin chào, tôi đang ở phòng 304 và tôi có một vấn đề.

**When to use:** Starting a polite complaint or problem report.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> I'm staying in room + number + and I have a problem.

**Usage note:** This sounds calm and gives the staff useful information first.

## 9. Could someone come and check it?

**Vietnamese:** Có ai có thể đến kiểm tra giúp tôi không?

**When to use:** Asking staff to inspect or fix a problem.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> Could someone + verb...?

**Usage note:** Use `it` when the problem has already been mentioned.

## 10. Is it possible to change rooms?

**Vietnamese:** Có thể đổi phòng được không?

**When to use:** Asking to move to another room.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> Is it possible to + verb...?

**Usage note:** This is a polite, indirect request.

## 11. Could you store my luggage for a few hours?

**Vietnamese:** Bạn có thể giữ hành lý của tôi trong vài tiếng không?

**When to use:** Asking the hotel to keep your bags before check-in or after checkout.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> Could you + verb + my luggage...?

**Usage note:** `Luggage` is usually uncountable. Do not say `luggages`.

## 12. I'd like to check out, please.

**Vietnamese:** Tôi muốn trả phòng.

**When to use:** Leaving the hotel and returning the room key/card.

**Key pattern/chunk:**

> I'd like to + verb, please.

**Usage note:** Short, polite, and natural.

# 4. Reusable Sentence Patterns

## Pattern 1: I'd like to + verb...

**Purpose:** Make a polite request.

**Source example:** "I'd like to book a room."

New examples:

* I'd like to check out, please.
* I'd like to make a reservation.
* I'd like to book one more night.
* I'd like to see the bill, please.

## Pattern 2: Do you have any + plural noun + available?

**Purpose:** Ask if something is free or ready to use.

**Source example:** "Do you have any rooms available?"

New examples:

* Do you have any rooms available for tonight?
* Do you have any twin rooms available?
* Do you have any suites available?
* Do you have any parking spaces available?

## Pattern 3: Is + noun + included?

**Purpose:** Ask whether something is part of the price or service.

**Source example:** "Is Wi-Fi included?"

New examples:

* Is breakfast included?
* Is parking included?
* Is airport transportation included?
* Is room service included in the rate?

## Pattern 4: Could I have + noun?

**Purpose:** Request an item politely.

**Source example:** "Could I have another pillow?"

New examples:

* Could I have an extra towel?
* Could I have a blanket?
* Could I have the Wi-Fi password?
* Could I have a copy of the receipt?

## Pattern 5: The + noun + isn't working.

**Purpose:** Report that a device or service has a problem.

**Source example:** "The air conditioner isn't working."

New examples:

* The Wi-Fi isn't working.
* The TV isn't working.
* The key card isn't working.
* The heater isn't working.

## Pattern 6: Could someone + verb...?

**Purpose:** Ask hotel staff to send help.

**Source example:** "Could someone come and check it?"

New examples:

* Could someone help me with this?
* Could someone bring fresh towels?
* Could someone clean the room later?
* Could someone help me with my luggage?

## Pattern 7: Is it possible to + verb...?

**Purpose:** Make a polite indirect request.

**Source example:** "Is it possible to change rooms?"

New examples:

* Is it possible to check in early?
* Is it possible to have a late checkout?
* Is it possible to extend my stay?
* Is it possible to book one more night?

# 6. Shadowing Set

## 1. I'd like to book a room for two nights.

**Natural chunking:** I'd like to book a room / for two nights.

**Speaking focus:** Link `like to` smoothly: /laɪk tə/.

## 2. I have a reservation under the name Leo.

**Natural chunking:** I have a reservation / under the name Leo.

**Speaking focus:** Stress `reservation` and your name.

## 3. Is breakfast included in the rate?

**Natural chunking:** Is breakfast included / in the rate?

**Speaking focus:** Rising intonation because it is a yes/no question.

## 4. Could I have another pillow?

**Natural chunking:** Could I have / another pillow?

**Speaking focus:** `Could I` often links softly: /kʊd aɪ/.

## 5. The air conditioner isn't working.

**Natural chunking:** The air conditioner / isn't working.

**Speaking focus:** Stress the problem word: `working`.

## 6. Hi, I'm staying in room 304 and I have a problem.

**Natural chunking:** Hi, / I'm staying in room 304 / and I have a problem.

**Speaking focus:** Keep the tone calm and polite.

## 7. Could someone come and check it?

**Natural chunking:** Could someone / come and check it?

**Speaking focus:** Link `come and` naturally: /kʌm ən/.

## 8. I'd like to check out, please.

**Natural chunking:** I'd like to check out, / please.

**Speaking focus:** Polite falling intonation at the end.
