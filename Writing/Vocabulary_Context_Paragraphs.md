# Vocabulary Context Paragraphs for Review

Source file: `Vocabulary(1).md`

## How to use this file

1. Read one paragraph aloud.
2. Underline the target vocabulary you remember.
3. Translate the paragraph into Vietnamese without looking up the words.
4. Cover the paragraph and retell the main idea in your own English.
5. Review the **Vocabulary covered** line to check whether you understood each word in context.

> Note: The paragraphs are intentionally dense because their purpose is vocabulary review, not natural storytelling only. Target words are shown in **bold**. Useful word-family forms are included where they fit naturally.

---

## Paragraph 1 — Urban transport and environmental policy

During the **transition** from car-dependent cities to cleaner urban planning, reliable public **transit** can **alleviate** traffic pressure and **ease** the daily burden on commuters. Governments can also **mitigate** pollution by taxing **single-use** plastic packaging and encouraging reusable products. These **transitional** policies may feel difficult at first, but they are not merely **transitory** solutions; if implemented carefully, they can reduce emissions and create healthier cities.

**Vocabulary covered:** transit, transition, alleviate, ease, mitigate, single-use  
**Useful word family:** transitional, transitory, alleviation, mitigation  
**Vietnamese gist:** Giao thông công cộng và chính sách giảm nhựa dùng một lần giúp giảm ùn tắc, ô nhiễm và tạo thành phố lành mạnh hơn.

---

## Paragraph 2 — Education, training, and student support

Many students cannot afford high **tuition**, so universities should expand **tuition remission**, scholarships, and monthly **stipends** for low-income learners. At the same time, **vocational** education should not be treated as inferior to academic study because it can protect young people’s **livelihoods** and help them find stable work. Good **faculty** members should judge students on **merit**, not background, and design **coursework** that rewards understanding rather than **rote** memorisation.

**Vocabulary covered:** vocational, tuition, livelihood, rote, coursework, merit, faculty, remission, stipend  
**Useful word family:** tutor, tutorial, vocation, vocationally, meritorious, merit-based, remit, remitted  
**Vietnamese gist:** Giáo dục nghề, miễn giảm học phí, trợ cấp và đánh giá dựa trên năng lực có thể giúp sinh viên có cơ hội công bằng hơn.

---

## Paragraph 3 — Agriculture and resource protection

In rural areas, farmers need reliable systems to **irrigate** crops, but poor planning can lead to the **overexploitation** of rivers and underground water. A small chemical **spill** from a factory can damage **pristine** land, while plastic **refuse** and household **detergent** can pollute streams if waste management is weak. To restore damaged soil, communities may **revegetate** hillsides, prevent further **exploitation**, and refuse to accept policies that ignore long-term ecological harm.

**Vocabulary covered:** irrigate, overexploitation, spill, detergent, pristine, refuse, revegetate  
**Useful word family:** irrigation, irrigated, overexploit, exploitation, spilled, spillage, revegetation  
**Vietnamese gist:** Nông nghiệp cần nước tưới, nhưng khai thác quá mức và ô nhiễm hóa chất có thể phá hủy đất và nguồn nước.

---

## Paragraph 4 — Politics, lobbying, and public responsibility

Environmental groups often **lobby** the government for stronger rules because surveillance data can reveal how **pervasive** pollution has become. When companies are **liable** for illegal dumping, public **accountability** improves, and officials are less likely to make **pointless** promises. **Eminent** scientists may **condemn** weak regulation and argue that modern pollution is almost **omnipresent** in some industrial zones.

**Vocabulary covered:** lobby, accountability, surveillance, condemn, omnipresent, eminent, pervasive, pointless, liable, almost  
**Useful word family:** lobbyist, lobbying, accountable, surveil, liability, condemnation  
**Vietnamese gist:** Vận động chính sách, giám sát và trách nhiệm giải trình giúp kiểm soát ô nhiễm và buộc doanh nghiệp chịu trách nhiệm.

---

## Paragraph 5 — Crime, punishment, and rehabilitation

In crime policy, punishment should **deter** serious **offences**, but a **proportionate sentence** is usually more effective than extreme **incarceration**. If prisons only punish offenders without education or counselling, **reoffending** may continue after release. Therefore, the justice system should **condemn** violent crime while still helping offenders reform, **attain** new skills, and avoid being permanently **labelled** as criminals.

**Vocabulary covered:** deter, reoffending, incarceration, proportionate sentence, offence, condemn, attain, label  
**Useful word family:** deterrent, deterrence, reoffend, offender, incarcerated, attainment, labelled  
**Vietnamese gist:** Hình phạt cần tương xứng và có tính răn đe, nhưng phục hồi và giáo dục cũng quan trọng để giảm tái phạm.

## Paragraph 6 — Social judgement and stereotypes

**Most** people say they value fairness, but they may still **stereotype** others based on appearance, accent, or family background. A student from a poor area might be **accused** of lacking discipline, while a rich student might be **labelled** as naturally talented. These unfair labels can create **hostile** classroom environments and **fierce** **rivalry**, even when the real problem is unequal access to support.

**Vocabulary covered:** most, stereotype, accuse, label, hostile, fierce, rivalry  
**Useful word family:** stereotypical, accusation, accused, hostility, fiercely, rival  
**Vietnamese gist:** Định kiến và quy chụp có thể tạo môi trường học tập thù địch và cạnh tranh không lành mạnh.

---

## Paragraph 7 — Childhood development and family relationships

During **adolescence**, young people gradually move toward **adulthood**, but their **upbringing** still shapes their values and emotional habits. A **close-knit** family can **forge** a strong **bond** between parents and children, while a weak parent-child **relation** may increase **tension** at home. **Fatherhood**, **motherhood**, **maternal** care, and everyday **affection** can help children **endure** stress and develop a more stable **temperament**.

**Vocabulary covered:** adolescence, adulthood, upbringing, close-knit, forge, bond, relation, tension, fatherhood, motherhood, maternal, affection, endure, temperament  
**Useful word family:** adolescent, adult, relational, endurance, affectionate  
**Vietnamese gist:** Gia đình gắn bó, sự chăm sóc của cha mẹ và tình cảm giúp trẻ vượt qua căng thẳng và phát triển cảm xúc ổn định.

## Paragraph 8 — Child behaviour and imagination

An **infant** may first **crawl** across the floor by **instinct**, while a **toddler** may suddenly have a **tantrum** when tired or hungry. Some children create **make-believe** worlds and **spontaneously** invent stories, but an overly **indulgent** parent may fail to set limits. As children grow, they may **rebel**, become more **conscious** of rules, or feel **self-conscious** when adults compare them with others.

**Vocabulary covered:** instinct, crawl, indulgent, infant, make-believe, rebel, conscious, self-conscious, spontaneously, tantrum, toddler  
**Useful word family:** instinctive, rebellious, consciousness, self-consciousness, spontaneity  
**Vietnamese gist:** Trẻ nhỏ phát triển qua bản năng, trí tưởng tượng, cảm xúc bùng phát và dần ý thức hơn về bản thân.

## Paragraph 9 — Adoption, resemblance, and emotional bonds

When a family decides to **adopt** a child, love is not determined by physical **resemblance**. What matters is whether parents can provide safety, **joy**, patience, and a stable emotional **bond**. In a **close-knit** adoptive family, children may not share genes with their parents, but they can still develop deep **affection** and a strong sense of belonging.

**Vocabulary covered:** adopt, resemblance, joy, bond, close-knit, affection  
**Useful word family:** adoption, adopted, joyful, resemble, affectionate  
**Vietnamese gist:** Việc nhận con nuôi cho thấy tình yêu gia đình không chỉ dựa trên sự giống nhau về ngoại hình hay huyết thống.

## Paragraph 10 — Medical emergency after a crash

After the **tragic** crash, rescue workers searched through the **wreckage** and found a patient with severe pain in the **abdomen**. Doctors used a portable **apparatus** to monitor each vital **organ** and decided to **alternate** between scans and physical checks. The patient had a poor **appetite**, felt **dizzy**, and needed treatment **briskly** because the injuries could become life-threatening.

**Vocabulary covered:** tragic, wreckage, abdomen, apparatus, organ, alternate, appetite, dizzy, briskly  
**Useful word family:** tragedy, abdominal, alternation, organic, dizziness, dizzily  
**Vietnamese gist:** Sau tai nạn nghiêm trọng, bác sĩ kiểm tra vùng bụng, cơ quan nội tạng và dùng thiết bị y tế để xử lý nhanh.

## Paragraph 11 — Health risks and medical treatment

A clear **onset** of symptoms, such as an **elevated** fever or **acute** pain, can signal a serious illness. Some patients have a genetic **predisposition** to disease, while others develop **debilitating** conditions because they **skip meals**, suffer from **insomnia**, or ignore early warning signs. A good doctor should provide a **clear-cut** diagnosis, offer a possible **cure**, and explain **consequently** what lifestyle changes are needed.

**Vocabulary covered:** acute, elevated, cure, clear-cut, consequently, debilitating, insomnia, onset, predisposition, skip meals  
**Useful word family:** acutely, elevation, curable, consequence, debilitate, sleeplessness, predisposed  
**Vietnamese gist:** Triệu chứng đột ngột, sốt cao, đau cấp tính, mất ngủ và bỏ bữa có thể dẫn đến vấn đề sức khỏe nghiêm trọng.

## Paragraph 12 — Public health and controversial choices

Public health campaigns often try to **curb** obesity by teaching people not to **skip meals** and not to eat oversized **servings** of processed food. However, these campaigns can become **controversial** if they seem to blame individuals rather than address poverty, stress, or lack of healthy options. People are **apt** to choose cheap food when they are busy, and **consequently**, better food education must be combined with affordable meals.

**Vocabulary covered:** curb, serving, controversial, apt, consequently, skip meals  
**Useful word family:** controversy, aptitude, serving size, curbing  
**Vietnamese gist:** Chính sách sức khỏe cần giảm béo phì nhưng không nên chỉ đổ lỗi cho cá nhân; cần giáo dục và thực phẩm lành mạnh dễ tiếp cận.

## Paragraph 13 — Mental health, marriage, and emotional support

In a stressful **marital** relationship, the **onset** of **insomnia** may **tempt** a person to rely on alcohol or unhealthy coping habits. If the stress becomes **debilitating**, family members should take warning signs seriously, especially when someone expresses **suicidal** thoughts or mentions **suicide**. A gentle **pat** on the shoulder, a calm conversation, or simply listening can help, but professional support is essential when risk is serious.

**Vocabulary covered:** marital, onset, insomnia, tempt, debilitating, suicidal, suicide, pat  
**Useful word family:** marriage, temptation, tempting, patted, patting  
**Vietnamese gist:** Căng thẳng hôn nhân và mất ngủ có thể ảnh hưởng nghiêm trọng đến sức khỏe tinh thần; cần hỗ trợ chuyên môn khi có dấu hiệu nguy hiểm.

## Paragraph 14 — Surgery, recovery, and physical comfort

After a kidney **transplant**, nurses may **rub** cream into dry skin, **pat** the wound area carefully, and help the patient **lean** against pillows. If the patient feels **dizzy**, has pain in the **abdomen**, or loses **appetite**, the medical team must respond quickly. Successful **transplantation** depends not only on the surgeon’s skill but also on careful recovery and emotional reassurance.

**Vocabulary covered:** transplant, rub, pat, lean, dizzy, abdomen, appetite  
**Useful word family:** transplantation, transplanted, rubbing, rubbed, patted, dizziness, abdominal  
**Vietnamese gist:** Sau phẫu thuật cấy ghép, bệnh nhân cần chăm sóc nhẹ nhàng, theo dõi chóng mặt, đau bụng và khả năng ăn uống.

## Paragraph 15 — Technology, privacy, and law

In modern cities, **surveillance** cameras are everywhere, and some people argue that this **omnipresent** monitoring can **deter** crime. Others **oppose** mass surveillance because it may violate privacy and make innocent citizens feel **self-conscious** in public spaces. A balanced law should make authorities **accountable**, prevent abuse, and ensure that any punishment for privacy-related **offences** remains proportionate.

**Vocabulary covered:** surveillance, omnipresent, deter, oppose, self-conscious, accountable, offence  
**Useful word family:** surveil, deterrence, opposition, accountability, offender  
**Vietnamese gist:** Camera giám sát có thể răn đe tội phạm nhưng cần luật cân bằng để bảo vệ quyền riêng tư.

## Paragraph 16 — Business competition and the underdog

A small start-up is often the **underdog** when it enters a market dominated by **eminent** corporations with **unparalleled** resources. Yet the company may still **attain** success if it offers a product with real **merit**, builds a loyal community, and refuses to be limited by unfair **stereotypes**. Its story can bring **joy** to customers who like seeing weaker competitors challenge powerful rivals.

**Vocabulary covered:** underdog, eminent, unparalleled, attain, merit, stereotype, joy  
**Useful word family:** underdogs, meritorious, merit-based, attainment, stereotypical, joyful  
**Vietnamese gist:** Một công ty yếu thế vẫn có thể thành công nếu sản phẩm có giá trị thật và không bị giới hạn bởi định kiến.

## Paragraph 17 — Family conflict and reconciliation

A **fierce** argument can damage a family **relation**, especially when parents **accuse** teenagers of laziness and teenagers **rebel** against strict rules. Such **hostile** exchanges may create long-term **tension**, but open communication can **ease** anger and help both sides **forge** a healthier bond. In many families, the conflict is not **pointless** if it leads to clearer expectations and more mutual respect.

**Vocabulary covered:** fierce, relation, accuse, rebel, hostile, tension, ease, forge, pointless  
**Useful word family:** accusation, rebellious, hostility, relational, forging  
**Vietnamese gist:** Mâu thuẫn gia đình có thể căng thẳng nhưng nếu giao tiếp tốt thì có thể tạo quan hệ lành mạnh hơn.

## Paragraph 18 — School reform and learning methods

Education reform should not simply **adopt** new exams and **label** them as progress. If students rely only on **rote** learning, they may complete **coursework** but fail to develop a deep understanding. Teachers should **alternate** between lectures, discussion, and projects so that learners can **attain** knowledge with ease and avoid becoming **self-conscious** when they make mistakes.

**Vocabulary covered:** adopt, label, rote, coursework, alternate, attain, ease, self-conscious  
**Useful word family:** adoption, labelled, alternation, attainment, easily, memorisation  
**Vietnamese gist:** Cải cách giáo dục nên giảm học vẹt, tăng thảo luận và dự án để học sinh hiểu sâu hơn.

## Paragraph 19 — Environmental law and corporate liability

When a company causes an oil **spill**, it should be **liable** for cleanup costs and required to restore **pristine** habitats. Regulators should **curb** **overexploitation**, **condemn** illegal dumping, and use **surveillance** evidence to hold managers **accountable**. If penalties are too weak, they may not act as a **deterrent**, and companies may continue to treat environmental damage as a business expense.

**Vocabulary covered:** spill, liable, pristine, curb, overexploitation, condemn, surveillance, accountable, deterrent  
**Useful word family:** spillage, liability, curbing, exploitation, condemnation, accountability, deter  
**Vietnamese gist:** Doanh nghiệp gây ô nhiễm phải chịu trách nhiệm và bị răn đe đủ mạnh để tránh tái phạm.

## Paragraph 20 — Childhood memory and personality

Looking back on her **upbringing**, she realised that her **temperament** was shaped by both strict rules and **maternal** warmth. As a child, she was an **indulgent** dreamer who loved **make-believe** games, but during **adolescence** she became more **conscious** of social judgment. By **adulthood**, she had learned to **endure** criticism without losing her natural **affection** for people.

**Vocabulary covered:** upbringing, temperament, maternal, indulgent, make-believe, adolescence, conscious, adulthood, endure, affection  
**Useful word family:** upbringing, temperamental, motherly, indulgence, consciousness, endurance, affectionate  
**Vietnamese gist:** Tính cách của một người được hình thành từ cách nuôi dạy, sự ấm áp của mẹ, trí tưởng tượng và trải nghiệm tuổi thiếu niên.

## Paragraph 21 — Ancient life and modern nutrition

In **prehistoric** times, humans did not worry about calories or exact **serving** sizes; hunger and **appetite** were guided mostly by survival needs. Today, however, people may **skip meals**, then eat too much later, which can make them feel **dizzy** or tired. Nutrition education should therefore teach practical habits rather than offer **pat** answers that ignore modern stress and busy schedules.

**Vocabulary covered:** prehistoric, serving, appetite, skip meals, dizzy, pat  
**Useful word family:** prehistory, servings, dizziness  
**Vietnamese gist:** Thói quen ăn uống hiện đại khác xa thời tiền sử, nên giáo dục dinh dưỡng cần thực tế hơn.

## Paragraph 22 — Medical technology and internal examination

A steerable medical **apparatus** can help doctors inspect a small **organ** inside the body without causing unnecessary harm. For example, a camera may move through the digestive system, examine the **abdomen**, and allow doctors to **steer** it toward hard-to-reach areas. This technology is **unparalleled** in some procedures because it gives a clearer view and may **mitigate** the need for more invasive surgery.

**Vocabulary covered:** apparatus, organ, abdomen, steer, unparalleled, mitigate  
**Useful word family:** steering, abdominal, mitigation  
**Vietnamese gist:** Thiết bị y tế có thể điều khiển hướng giúp bác sĩ quan sát cơ quan bên trong cơ thể và giảm nhu cầu phẫu thuật xâm lấn.

## Paragraph 23 — Work, stress, and health decline

A worker who tries to finish every task **briskly** may seem productive, but constant pressure can become **debilitating**. If insomnia begins at the **onset** of a stressful project, the person may **lean** on caffeine, lose **appetite**, and become too **dizzy** to focus. Employers should **alleviate** pressure with realistic deadlines rather than waiting until burnout becomes **acute**.

**Vocabulary covered:** briskly, debilitating, onset, insomnia, lean, appetite, dizzy, alleviate, acute  
**Useful word family:** brisk, debilitation, dizziness, alleviation, acutely  
**Vietnamese gist:** Làm việc quá căng thẳng có thể gây mất ngủ, chóng mặt và suy nhược, nên công ty cần giảm áp lực sớm.

---

## Paragraph 24 — Public debate and clear thinking

A **controversial** policy is not always wrong; sometimes it deserves serious discussion on its **merits**. However, debate becomes weak when people **accuse** opponents unfairly, rely on **stereotypes**, or use emotional labels instead of evidence. A **clear-cut** argument should explain causes and consequences, show why a proposal may **mitigate** harm, and admit when the evidence is **almost** but not completely certain.

**Vocabulary covered:** controversial, merit, accuse, stereotype, clear-cut, mitigate, almost  
**Useful word family:** controversy, meritorious, accusation, stereotypical, mitigation  
**Vietnamese gist:** Chính sách gây tranh cãi vẫn có thể có giá trị nếu tranh luận dựa trên bằng chứng thay vì quy chụp.

## Paragraph 25 — Final integrated review paragraph

A society cannot solve every problem with one policy: public **transit** may reduce pollution, **vocational** training may protect **livelihoods**, fair courts may reduce **reoffending**, and family **affection** may help children grow into stable adults. Yet each solution requires **accountability**, careful planning, and the courage to **oppose** harmful habits, whether they involve **single-use** waste, **rote** learning, unfair **labels**, or the **overexploitation** of nature. In the end, progress is rarely **clear-cut**, but consistent effort can **ease** suffering, **mitigate** risks, and help people **attain** a better life.

**Vocabulary covered:** transit, vocational, livelihoods, reoffending, affection, accountability, oppose, single-use, rote, labels, overexploitation, clear-cut, ease, mitigate, attain  
**Useful word family:** accountable, opposition, labelled, exploitation, mitigation, attainment  
**Vietnamese gist:** Xã hội cần nhiều giải pháp kết hợp: giao thông, giáo dục nghề, tư pháp công bằng, gia đình, môi trường và trách nhiệm giải trình.

## Extra paragraph — Stroke

A **stroke** can happen suddenly when blood flow to part of the brain is blocked, so doctors must act quickly to reduce long-term damage. After a stroke, some patients need rehabilitation, gentle exercise, and family support to rebuild confidence. The word **stroke** can also mean a gentle movement of the hand, as in “she stroked the cat,” but in medical contexts it usually refers to a serious brain event.

**Vocabulary covered:** stroke  
**Useful word family:** stroked, stroke patient  
**Vietnamese gist:** Stroke có thể nghĩa là đột quỵ trong y khoa hoặc vuốt nhẹ trong đời thường.
